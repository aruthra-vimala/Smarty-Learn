Acid-Base Indicator

Acid-base testing is a method used to determine whether a substance is acidic, basic, or neutral by using special chemical compounds called indicators. These indicators change color based on the pH of the solution, helping to identify its nature.

Testing Procedure:

1. Take a small sample of the unknown solution.

2. Add a few drops of the chosen indicator.

3. Observe the color change and compare it with a pH scale.

4. Determine whether the solution is acidic, basic, or neutral.
