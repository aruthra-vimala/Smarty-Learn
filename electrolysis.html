Electrolysis of Water

Electrolysis is a process that uses electricity to break down a compound into its elements. This technique is widely used in industries for extracting and refining metals, producing chemicals, and even splitting water into hydrogen and oxygen.

Electrolysis occurs in a solution or molten state, where the substance being decomposed (called the electrolyte) allows electric current to pass through. The process requires two key components:

Anode (+): The positive electrode

Cathode (-): The negative electrode

When an electric current is passed through the electrolyte, ions move toward their respective electrodes:

Cations (positive ions) move toward the cathode, where they gain electrons (reduction).

Anions (negative ions) move toward the anode, where they lose electrons (oxidation).
